About Me

Art has always featured in my life, having parents whose hobbies were painting and gardening. I was lucky to get a brief exposure to the world of ceramics during my teen years this was followed many years later by two years of evening classes at Lincoln Art College. On retirement from the NHS I joined a regular class at the Oxcombe Ceramic Studio in the Wolds where we exchange ideas and discuss ceramics most weeks.

My home studio was developed during the Covid Crisis which was the catalyst to bring forward plans for converting part of our cartshed / garage into a studio. Teaching was always part of my NHS life including an MSc in adult education completed through Sheffield Hallam Uni. Therefore, the studio was designed with room for two wheels and the option of some further teaching / demonstrating of a non clinical nature. Most (but not all) of the work created in the studio is intended to have a functional use but also to hopefully be aesthetically pleasing and tactile.

I think all those working with ceramics marvel at the infinite variety of glaze finishes that result from the mix of “earth and fire” this is undoubtedly the attraction, charm and frustration of pottery. We gain inspiration from other potters and from interactions in life and the natural world. Please feel free to contact me at my email below if you want “to have a go”.
