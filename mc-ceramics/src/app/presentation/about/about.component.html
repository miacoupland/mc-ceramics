About Me

Art has always featured in my life, I was lucky to have a brief exposure to pottery firstly in my teens then this was followed many years later by two years of evening classes at Lincoln Art College. On retirement from the NHS i joined a regular ceramic art class at the Oxcombe studio in the Lincolnshire Wolds where we exchange ideas and discuss ceramics and techniques most weeks.

My home studio was developed and constructed during the Covid Crisis which was the catalyst to bring forward plans to convert part of our garage / cartshed into a small art studio. Before retirement teaching was a major part of my clinical role so the studio was designed with enough room for two potters wheels and the option to do some more teaching and demonstrating - an ideal environment to encourage people to have a go at throwing, building and sculpting. Most ( but not all ) of the work created is stoneware pottery with a functional use but there is the opportunity for pieces to be aesthetically pleasing and tactile.

I think all those working with ceramics marvel at the infinite variety of glaze finishes that result from the mix of “Earth and Fire” this is undoubtedly the attraction, charm, and at times the frustration of pottery. Potters gain inspiration from each other, the natural world and interactions in life.

Please feel free to contact me at michaelcoupland4@icloud.com if you want to have a go. 3 Hour sessions for two people are charged at £50, for one person £30. A follow on session can then be arranged for glazing turning etc if wished.
